# Friday — Brand Bible

**Domain:** itsfriday.dev
**Product:** A branded client portal for freelance designers and dev-designer hybrids
**Tagline:** *It's Friday. Every day.*

---

## 1. Brand Origin Story

You didn't quit your job because you hated working. You quit because you hated working like *that*. The fluorescent lights. The standup meetings about standup meetings. The tools that felt like they were designed by people who'd never freelanced a day in their lives.

You went independent because you wanted to design from a café in Lakeland, a coworking space in San José, or your back porch at 6am with coffee and no Slack notifications. You wanted every day to feel like Friday — that exhale at the end of the week when the work is shipped, the client is happy, and the laptop closes.

But then you discovered the ugly truth of freelancing: the *business* of freelancing is chaos. Client files scattered across Google Drive. Feedback buried in email threads. Invoices you forget to send. A Notion page you swear you shared but didn't. Tools like HoneyBook that charge you $50/month and still can't show a client where their project stands.

**Friday exists because your clients deserve the same quality experience as your designs.** A calm, beautiful, branded space where they can see their project status, review your work, leave feedback right on the mockup, and pay you — without a single "just checking in" email.

Friday is the tool you'd build for yourself. Because that's exactly what happened.

## 3. Brand Persona

If Friday were a person, they'd be:

**The freelancer who figured it out.** Not a guru, not a hustle-culture bro, not a "passive income" influencer. Just someone who's been freelancing for 5+ years, has 8-12 steady clients, works from wherever they want, and has their systems dialed. They're generous with what they've learned. They don't flex — they just deliver.

### Personality Traits

| Trait | Expression |
|-------|-----------|
| **Confident, not arrogant** | "We built this because nothing else worked. You'll see." |
| **Warm, not fluffy** | Friendly but direct. No corporate filler. No "we're so excited to announce." |
| **Minimal, not cold** | Clean design, clear copy, but always human. Never sterile. |
| **Opinionated, not preachy** | "You don't need 47 features. You need 7 that actually work." |
| **Independent, not isolated** | Celebrates the freelance community. You're solo, not alone. |

### Brand Archetypes

**Primary: The Creator** — Design-obsessed, quality-driven, cares about craft
**Secondary: The Explorer** — Location-independent, curious, freedom-seeking

## 4. Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice (consistent always)
- First person plural ("we") when talking about Friday the product
- Second person ("you") when talking to the customer — they're the hero, not us
- Short sentences. Fragments are fine. We write like we talk.
- No jargon, no buzzwords, no "leverage" or "synergy" or "empower"
- Lowercase energy in casual contexts (social media, onboarding). Proper in formal (legal, invoices).

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Landing page** | Confident + warm | "Your clients deserve better than a Google Drive link." |
| **Onboarding** | Encouraging + practical | "Let's get your first client portal live. Takes about 4 minutes." |
| **Error states** | Calm + helpful | "That file didn't upload. Try again, or drag a smaller one in." |
| **Marketing/social** | Playful + relatable | "It's 2026 and you're still emailing PNGs for approval. We can fix that." |
| **Invoicing** | Professional + clean | "Invoice #1042 — due April 21, 2026" |
| **Changelog** | Direct + excited | "Design review just got threaded replies. Pin a comment, start a conversation." |

### Words We Use
- Ship, deliver, share, review, approve
- Clean, simple, fast, calm, clear
- Portal, space, project, client
- Freedom, independent, anywhere

### Words We Don't Use
- Empower, leverage, synergy, disrupt
- Hustle, grind, scale (unless ironic)
- Enterprise, solution, robust, comprehensive
- "We're so excited to announce" / "We're thrilled"

## 7. Visual Identity Direction

### Logo Concept

**Primary mark:** A clean wordmark — "friday" in lowercase, custom-kerned. The dot over the 'i' is subtly replaced with a small sun or horizon line, suggesting the dawn of freedom / the golden hour. Minimal enough to work at 16px favicon size.

**Icon mark (for favicon/app icon):** The letter "F" stylized with the same sun/horizon dot. Or a simple abstract mark — a rounded square with a horizon line, suggesting a window (portal) with a sunset.

**Logo rules:**
- Always lowercase "friday" in the wordmark
- Generous spacing around the mark
- Never stretch, rotate, or recolor outside the approved palette

### Color Palette

**Core philosophy:** Warm neutrals + one signature accent. The palette should feel like golden hour on a Friday afternoon — warm, calm, optimistic.

**Primary colors:**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Midnight** | #1A1A2E | Primary text, dark backgrounds |
| **Warm White** | #FAFAF7 | Page backgrounds, cards |
| **Sand** | #F0EBE3 | Secondary backgrounds, subtle fills |

**Accent color:**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Golden** | #E8A838 | CTAs, highlights, the "Friday feeling" |
| **Sunset** | #D4724E | Secondary accent, warm notifications |

**Supporting colors:**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Sage** | #7C9082 | Success states, approved badges |
| **Slate** | #6B7280 | Secondary text, borders, muted UI |
| **Coral** | #E85D5D | Error states, overdue invoices |

**Dark mode:** Midnight becomes the base. Warm White inverts to a soft dark (#1E1E2A). Golden stays golden — it's the constant across both modes.

### Typography

**Primary (headings):** A clean geometric sans-serif with personality. Candidates:
- **Satoshi** — modern, geometric, widely loved by designers. Free.
- **General Sans** — similar energy, slightly warmer. Free.
- **Plus Jakarta Sans** — rounded, friendly, professional. Free on Google Fonts.

**Secondary (body):** A highly readable sans-serif:
- **Inter** — the workhorse. Every designer has it. Pairs well with anything.
- Or use the primary font at regular weight for body too (single-font system for simplicity).

**Mono (code/data):** JetBrains Mono or Fira Code for invoice numbers, project IDs, and any data display.

**Type scale:**
- Hero: 48-64px / bold
- H1: 36px / semibold
- H2: 24px / semibold
- H3: 18px / medium
- Body: 16px / regular
- Small: 14px / regular
- Caption: 12px / regular

### Imagery & Illustration Style

- **No stock photos.** Ever. If we show people, they're real freelancers (UGC, testimonials with real photos, or tasteful lifestyle shots we create).
- **Abstract shapes** over literal illustrations. Soft gradients, organic blobs, subtle grain texture — warm and calm, not corporate.
- **Screenshots are the hero.** The product itself is the best marketing asset. Show the actual UI, beautifully framed in device mockups.
- **Minimal iconography.** Line icons only (Lucide icon set), consistent 1.5px stroke weight.

### Motion & Interaction

- Subtle, purposeful transitions (150-300ms ease-out)
- No bouncy animations, no confetti, no "delightful" loading spinners
- Page transitions: gentle fade + slight upward drift
- Button hover: subtle background fill, never scale/bounce
- The product should feel like sliding into a warm chair, not riding a rollercoaster

## 10. Brand Story — The Long Version (for About page)

### The story of Friday

We canceled HoneyBook on a Tuesday.

Not because it was bad — it was fine. Fine for wedding photographers. Fine for event planners. Fine for people who book clients once and move on. But we're designers. Our work is iterative. Clients need to see progress, leave feedback on mockups, download deliverables, and check project status without sending us a "just checking in!" email at 11pm.

HoneyBook didn't do any of that. Neither did Dubsado, or Bonsai, or the twelve other tools we tried. They all stopped being useful the moment a contract was signed. So we did what every freelance designer eventually does: we duct-taped together Notion, Google Drive, Slack, and Stripe into a Frankenstein client experience and hoped nobody noticed.

They noticed.

Friday started as the tool we built for ourselves. A simple, branded portal where clients could see exactly where their project stood, review designs with pin-drop comments instead of paragraph-long emails, download final files, and pay invoices — all in one place that looked like it was designed by someone who gives a damn.

Because it was.

We charge $14/month. No platform transaction fees. No setup that takes weeks. No features you'll never use. Just the seven things freelance designers actually need, done well.

Monday is for the 9-to-5. Friday is for the rest of us.
