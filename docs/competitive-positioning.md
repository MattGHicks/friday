## The one-line position

> Friday is the only client portal built *for designers* — with visual design review at the center, not bolted on.

---

## The competitive landscape

Freelance designers currently default to one of four tools (or a messy mix of all of them):

| Tool | Primary pitch | Who it's for |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| **HoneyBook** | All-in-one business platform | Photographers, event planners, creative services broadly |
| **Dubsado** | Customizable CRM + workflows | Highly organized freelancers willing to invest setup time |
| **Clientjoy** | Sales pipeline + proposals + billing | Agencies and B2B consultants |
| **Friday** | Branded client portal with design review | Freelance brand/UI/graphic designers, 1–5 yrs |

---

## Head-to-head breakdown

### vs. HoneyBook

**HoneyBook's strengths**
- Strong brand recognition in creative freelancer market
- Robust proposal + contract + invoice workflow
- Client booking/scheduler (contact forms, lead captures)
- Automations (reminder emails, payment nudges)
- Established community (Facebook group ~50k members)

**HoneyBook's weaknesses**
- No visual design review. Feedback happens in email.
- Client portal is generic, unbranded, cluttered
- Designed for photographers and event planners — not visual deliverable workflows
- Price: $36/mo (Starter), $66/mo (Essentials) — expensive for solo designers
- Complexity creep: users complain setup takes weeks
- Mobile app feels like an afterthought
- Not design-forward — the product doesn't look like something a designer would use

**Friday's counter-position vs HoneyBook**
> "HoneyBook runs your business. Friday impresses your clients."
> Friday is lean and fast to set up. HoneyBook requires a weekend.
> Friday has pin-drop annotation. HoneyBook has email threads.
> Friday's client portal looks like your work. HoneyBook's looks like HoneyBook's.

**Key SEO/messaging angle:** "HoneyBook alternative for designers" — target designers frustrated by HoneyBook's complexity and lack of design-review features.

---

### vs. Dubsado

**Dubsado's strengths**
- Highly customizable (forms, contracts, automations, canned emails)
- Strong among designers who want to fully automate their onboarding
- White-label client portal (can embed custom domain)
- Affordable: $20/mo (Starter), $40/mo (Premier)
- Active user community, good documentation

**Dubsado's weaknesses**
- Notorious learning curve — the most common complaint is "took me 3 months to set up"
- Visual design feedback is not a feature at all
- Client portal is customizable but still generic in feel — looks like a form builder
- No native file review or annotation
- UI is dated and not design-forward
- Automations are powerful but brittle — easy to break

**Friday's counter-position vs Dubsado**
> "Dubsado is a platform you configure. Friday is a portal your clients actually love."
> Dubsado rewards the patient and the organized. Friday works on day one.
> If Dubsado is a CRM that happens to have a portal, Friday is a portal that happens to have invoicing.

**Key SEO/messaging angle:** "Dubsado alternative" — target burned-out Dubsado users who spent weeks setting it up and still get design feedback in email.

---

### vs. Clientjoy

**Clientjoy's strengths**
- Strong pipeline/CRM focus (lead tracking, proposals, e-signatures)
- Affordable for small agencies: $23/mo for up to 3 users
- Clean UI, reasonably modern design
- Good for consultants and agencies who need sales pipeline visibility

**Clientjoy's weaknesses**
- Not focused on freelance designers — primarily B2B/agency tool
- No visual design review or annotation
- Client portal is a proposal/invoice delivery mechanism — not a collaborative space
- No file annotation, no design-native workflow
- Not built for delivering design work — built for selling services
- Smaller community, less social proof in designer circles

**Friday's counter-position vs Clientjoy**
> "Clientjoy manages your sales pipeline. Friday manages your client relationships."
> Clientjoy is built for agencies closing deals. Friday is built for designers delivering work.
> The handoff from proposal to project to delivery is Friday's whole job.

**Key SEO/messaging angle:** Less direct competition here. Clientjoy isn't the primary alternative designers consider — mostly an upsell target from spreadsheet users.

## Friday's unique wedge

Every competitor above handles contracts, invoices, and client communication to varying degrees. None of them do **design review**.

That's the wedge:

1. **Pin-drop annotation on uploaded images** — comment directly on the design
2. **Numbered pins** — reference specific feedback without ambiguity
3. **Threaded replies** — back-and-forth in context, not email
4. **Resolve/reopen** — mark feedback as addressed
5. **Approve / Request Changes** — clear review status per deliverable

This maps directly to the pain: *"My clients leave feedback in four different places, and I still can't find what they wanted changed on slide 3."*

## Where Friday is currently weaker (honest assessment)

| Gap | HoneyBook | Dubsado | Friday |
|-----|-----------|---------|--------|
| Contracts / e-signatures | ✅ | ✅ | ✗ (not built) |
| Automations / email sequences | ✅ | ✅ | ✗ |
| Scheduling / booking | ✅ | ✅ | ✗ |
| Lead capture forms | ✅ | ✅ | ✗ |
| Mobile app | ✅ | partial | ✗ |
| Established community | ✅ | ✅ | building |

**Positioning implication:** Friday isn't trying to replace all of HoneyBook or Dubsado on day one. The pitch is: *"Keep using whatever you use for contracts. Use Friday for everything that happens after you're hired — the portal, the reviews, the files, the invoices."*

This is a credible entry wedge. It asks designers to try one thing, not switch everything.
